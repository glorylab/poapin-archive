# Code of Conduct

POAPin Archive is built to preserve community history. The project should be a
place where people can do careful work without being diminished, harassed, or
excluded.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that strengthens the community include:

- being respectful across differences in experience, identity, language, and
  point of view;
- giving specific, actionable feedback about the work rather than the person;
- assuming good intent while remaining accountable for impact;
- acknowledging mistakes, correcting them, and learning in public; and
- treating data provenance, privacy, accessibility, and creators' rights as
  shared responsibilities.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, sexualized attention,
discriminatory language, sustained disruption, doxxing, publishing private
information, or retaliation against someone who raises a concern.

## Scope

This code applies in repository spaces and in public spaces when someone is
representing the project, including issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
community calls, and project-related direct messages.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to Kira at
[kira@glorylab.xyz](mailto:kira@glorylab.xyz). Include links, dates, context,
and any immediate safety concern. Reports will be handled as confidentially as
practical; information will be shared only when needed to investigate or
protect participants.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue a warning, temporarily limit
participation, or permanently ban a participant. Decisions will consider the
severity, pattern, context, and safety of the people involved. Retaliation for a
good-faith report is itself a violation.

## Good-faith disagreement

Technical disagreement, firm review, and declining a proposal are not conduct
violations by themselves. Keep the discussion tied to evidence, trade-offs, and
the project's stated constraints. If a conversation stops being productive,
maintainers may pause it and summarize the decision separately.
